Jackson Lab Summer Program

The Jackson Lab Summer Student Program is designed for students who want to immerse themselves in genetics and genomics research. It emphasizes laboratory discovery, communication of knowledge, and professional growth.
  • Students participate in an ongoing research program with the support of an experienced scientific mentor. They develop an independent project, implement their plan, analyze the data, and report the results. At the end of the summer, they present their findings to researchers, other students, and parents.

  • Admission is competitive, and students of all backgrounds are selected. All students receive a stipend of $6,000 for the 10-week program, including room and board at Highseas (Maine program) or at the University of St. Joseph (Connecticut program). The cost of round-trip travel between the student's home and the Laboratory is also provided.

  • The Jackson Lab Summer Student Program seeks to admit students from a wide range of backgrounds and experiences. These efforts enhance the impact of the SSP experience for all participants and support national efforts to expand the biomedical research workforce to individuals from groups underrepresented and underserved in biomedicine. The SSP will include a diverse community of learners. Students from groups underrepresented in the sciences are strongly encouraged to apply.

  • Here is the application for the Jackson Lab's paid summer science program. Note that only US Citizens and Permanent Residents can go. The deadline is Feb 2.

Student Leaders Program

Through Student Leaders, Bank of America recognizes high school students who lead and serve in their own communities and beyond. We connect Student Leaders to tools and resources to help make communities better.
  • Since 2004, Student Leaders has been part of our ongoing commitment to youth employment and economic mobility. Preparing a diverse pipeline of community-minded young students with the leadership training they need to be successful in the workforce is vital to our approach to responsible growth and helps to build thriving communities. Through our Student Leaders program we connect more than 300 community-minded high school juniors and seniors from nearly 100 communities to employment, skills development and service.

  • They're awarded paid summer internships with local nonprofits such as Boys and Girls Clubs of America and Habitat for Humanity and participate in a national leadership summit in Washington, D.C. The Student Leaders Summit focuses on skill building and creating a more civically engaged society.

  • Here is the application for the Student Leaders Program. The 2020 Student Leaders application will be open from November 4, 2019 – January 31, 2020

The MERITS Program

The MERITS (Maine Research Internships for Teachers and Students) Program provides summer research opportunities (6 weeks) for highly motivated high school students who are interested in science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) careers to experience “real-time” applications of STEM in a research-focused work world at host institutions conducting research and technology development.
  • The Maine Space Grant Consortium (MSGC) is pleased to announce the availability of funds to support a limited number of summer research internships for highly motivated Maine high school Juniors for the summer of 2020.

  • By doing so, MSGC seeks to expose students to career opportunities in Maine and to provide a direct way by which Maine businesses and research communities can attract young people with high potential for possible future employment.

  • Here is the application for the MERITS Program. Students must be U.S citizens, residents of Maine and must be enrolled at a Maine school to apply to this program. Applications are due January 15, 2020

Junior Volunteer Program

Thank you for your interest in the Junior Volunteer program at Maine Medical Center! The program offers high school students an opportunity to gain experience in a healthcare setting while learning new skills and giving back to their community.
  • Some areas Junior Volunteers may serve in include: retail shops, information desk, escort, cafeteria, admitting, hospital elder life, and other clinical areas as well as office environments throughout the hospital.

  • Junior Volunteers serving their first year in the program will likely be placed in a food services or retail setting for at least one of their two assignments. Assignment preference is given to Returning Junior Volunteers serving in their 3rd or 4th year. Placement in a specific hospital department cannot be guaranteed.

  • Here is the application for the Junior Volunteer Program. Students must be a high school student, entering freshman through senior year in fall 2020. Applications are due January 24, 2020

Summer Students Research Program

For seniors over 18 only! During the summer months Maine Medical Center Research Institute (MMCRI) provides undergraduates with an opportunity to receive an educational experience in biomedical science through the MMCRI Summer Student Research Program.
  • Research at MMCRI covers a broad area in biomedical science including vascular biology, stem cell biology, developmental biology, neurobiology, hematology, nephrology, tumor biology, and molecular genetics as well as outcomes research, vector-borne diseases, and clinical research.

  • The Summer Student program is ten weeks in duration, and students work 40 hours per week. The rate of pay for Summer Student Research program participants paid by Maine Medical Center is $13 per hour. Students are responsible for their own transportation and housing.

  • Here is the application for the Student Summer Research Program. Students must be at least 18 years of age. Applications are due February 11, 2020

Tips:

Tips for job a interview

  • Research the company and interviewers before going.

  • Practice answers to common interview questions with a friend of family member.

  • Dress to impress, wear business formal clothes.

  • Send a thank you letter afterwards to show your interest.

Tips for writing job an application

  • Sell yourself, write about your positive impacts on your community.

  • Take your time to make sure your application will stand out to the employers.

  • Emphasize previous jobs and other work that closely relates to the job you are applying to.

  • Focus on using power verbs and descriptive words to specifically answer the question at hand.
